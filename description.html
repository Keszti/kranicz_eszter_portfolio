How it was made

In my portfolio I wanted to showcase my digital artworks, therefore I found it essential to make my illustrations the center of attention. To achive this, I tried to come up with a clean and easily navigable layout design.

My initial concept was a little bit different than my final website, but overall I managed to stick with my original idea.

Formerly, the buttons of the navigation menu were meant to have a slight white border, however it did not work out with the mobile-friendly responsive layout, as it looked a bit crappy. So instead, I applied a light-blue glow effect on them, by the help of text shadow and radial-gradient.

I also had a little trouble with positioning and shrinking the text ’Illustration & concept art’, which appears on top of my background picture, below my signature. But in the end, thanks to W3schools (which was mentioned during the lessons) I was able to figure it out.

For inspiration I looked at multiple existing art portfolios, which are linked on the right. Although I tried to be original and develop a unique website, I still couldn't help picking up ideas from all the portfolios I've checked out. (For example linking social media sites in the footer.)
